11.13 Basics

Here are some tips based on common marker comments of how to write a decent assingmentâ€¦

  • Donâ€™t cut and paste maps / figures / formulas / tables, export them properly or remake them nicely and credit a source somewhere such as the figure caption (e.g.Â source Smith et al.Â 2009 or adapted from Smith et al.Â 2009).
  • Make maps and figures the right way â€” donâ€™t just leave layer names (e.g.Â osm_points_road) in a legend, rename them to something with meaning!
  • If you use a formula write it properly â€” donâ€™t cut and paste, fully explain all the symbols / variables too.

  • Donâ€™t bullet point methods, summarise with sentances and use a flow diagram if appropraite.

11.13.1 Introduction

Make sure you say why your mini investiation is important near the startâ€” why should we care! Try to answer the so what question. The introduction should set the scene and introduce the reader to issue (what is the background to it), its importance (so what?) and lead onto outlining the research question of the study.

11.13.2 Literature review

The literature review should evaluate exisiting research, demonstrate contrasting and/or similar views whilst highlight research gaps (that hopefully your research will contribute to / address). This could also include policy documents or documents from reputable soruces (e.g.Â UN / EU / authoritative bodies). Wikepedia is not a valid academic source and please donâ€™t just list what authors have done in the past (e.g.Â Smith 2009 did x but Jones 2008 did y then Frank 2010 did x). Synthesise previous work / policy documents and provide a narrative through it whilst trying to show where the research gap is / where you question fits in. Try to end the literature review with a concluding paragraph that concisely summarises everything within it and states what your work is going to contribute or address. Think of this section as providing a story to what everyone else has done (whilst also showing issues / reseach gaps) then at then end BAMMMâ€¦ this is what you are going to do.

11.13.3 Methodology

Methodology is what you did (analysis, data processing, data cleaning, etc), a reader should be able to replicate your analysis but you donâ€™t need excessive detail â€” you do not need to list every tool you used. For example, data was loaded uisng read_csv() and then reprojected using st_transform() is too much information for the assignment (you could include this type of information in the comments of your code / RMarkdown document).

11.13.4 Results

Results is what you found â€” be selective about figures you include and provide. To do so ask yourself what does this contribute to my research / aim / objective to see if you need it. Please provide a narrative that guides the reader through your findings, donâ€™t just present result after result after result after result.
